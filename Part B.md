How Boosting Differs from Bagging
While Bagging (used in Random Forest) builds multiple independent models in parallel and averages their results to reduce variance, Boosting builds models sequentially. In boosting, each new model attempts to correct the errors (residuals) made by the previous models in the sequence. This conversion of 'weak learners' into a 'strong learner' focuses on reducing bias and improving predictive accuracy, though it can be more sensitive to noise and outliers than bagging.
